Chemical Engineering

Chemical Engineering deals with the study of design and operation of chemical plants and methods of improving production. Chemical engineers develop economical commercial processes to convert raw material into useful products.
What do Chemical Engineers do?
Chemical engineers typically do the following:
  • Conduct research to develop new and improved manufacturing processes
  • Design and plan the layout of equipment
  • Work in the production of energy, electronics, food, clothing, and paper
  • Conduct research in the life sciences, biotechnology, and business services

  • Etc.
Brief history of Chemical Engineering
History. Chemical engineering is as old as the process industries. Its heritage dates from the fermentation and evaporation processes operated by early civilizations. Modern chemical engineering emerged with the development of large-scale, chemical-manufacturing operations in the second half of the 19th century.
As technology expanded so did the demand for chemical processes
The father of chemical engineering:
George E. Davis
Davis in the form of twelve lectures covering various aspects of industrial chemical practice. As a consequence George E. Davis is regarded as the world's first chemical engineer
Major notable acheivements come from:
  • Joseph Priestley (discoverer of oxygen)
  • Alfred Nobel (dynamite)
  • Dmitri Mendeleev (the periodic table)
  • Dorothy Hodgkin (X-ray Crystallography)
  • Etc.
What companies are currently leading the Chemical industry?
  • BASF - The current largest company - a german multinational cooropration responsible from chemicals, plastics, performance products and crop protection products to oil and gas.
  • Sinopec - Chinas 2.2 trillion dollar Petroleum & Chemical Corporation based in Beijing responsible for the production, refining and transportation of crude oil and natural gas.
  • Saudi Basic Industries Corporation - known as SABIC, is a Saudi Arabian multinational chemical manufacturing company. It is active in petrochemicals, chemicals, industrial polymers, fertilizers, and metals.
